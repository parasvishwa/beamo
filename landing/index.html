The Problem

Why screen sharing
usually fails

Current tools are ecosystem-locked, break on enterprise networks, and leave older devices completely unsupported.

Protocol fragmentation
AirPlay, Miracast, and Google Cast don't talk to each other. Mac to Android TV? No native path exists. You need a different app for every combination.
WifiScreen works across all ecosystems
Blocked on school networks
AirPlay requires Bonjour/mDNS multicast which is blocked on every enterprise, school, and guest WiFi network. It silently fails with no explanation.
Uses standard WebRTC - works everywhere HTTP works
Old devices left behind
AirPlay 2 on TVs requires Samsung 2018+, LG 2019+, Sony 2019+. Budget brands and older hardware get nothing. A massive installed base with no solution.
Any device with a browser works as a viewer
How it works

Three steps.
Under 30 seconds.

1
Open broadcaster on your Mac or PC
Go to the broadcaster page in Chrome or Edge. Click "Start Sharing" and select your screen in the browser dialog.
2
Viewers scan the QR code
A unique room code and QR code appears. Viewers scan it on any device or type the 6-character code at viewer.html.
3
Screen streams in real time
Viewers see your screen live via WebRTC. No buffering, no lag, encrypted peer-to-peer on your local WiFi.
