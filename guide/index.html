User Guide

How to share your screen with Beamo

Simple step-by-step guide. If you can open a website, you can use Beamo. No downloads, no accounts, no technical knowledge needed.

Broadcaster (the Mac/PC)
Needs Chrome or Edge browser. That's it. One click to start.
Viewers (any device)
Any phone, tablet, laptop, or TV with any browser. No install needed — ever.
Total setup time
Under 30 seconds from clicking Start Sharing to viewers seeing the screen.
Part 1 of 2

For the person sharing their screen

Do this on the Mac or PC whose screen you want to show to others.

1
Open Chrome or Edge browser
Screen sharing only works in Chrome or Edge. It will NOT work in Safari or Firefox. If you don't have Chrome, download it free at google.com/chrome.
If you see a yellow warning bar at the top of the page, you are in Safari — switch to Chrome.
2
Go to beamo.imazyn.com
Type beamo.imazyn.com in the address bar and press Enter. You will see the Beamo home page with a big cyan "Start Sharing" button.
3
Click "Start Sharing"
A dialog box will appear asking what you want to share. You can choose your entire screen, a specific window, or a browser tab.
For presentations, select "Entire Screen" so viewers see everything you do.
4
Share the Room Code or QR Code
After you click Share, a 6-character Room Code appears (like MX7K2P) along with a QR code. Tell viewers the code or let them scan the QR code with their phone camera.
Click "Copy link" to copy the viewer URL and paste it in WhatsApp, email, or a chat — viewers just tap the link.
5
Done — your screen is live!
The page shows how many viewers are connected. When you are finished, click "Stop Sharing" or just close the browser tab.
Part 2 of 2

For viewers (anyone watching)

Do this on any phone, tablet, laptop, or smart TV. No app install needed.

1
Option A — Scan the QR Code
Open your phone camera and point it at the QR code on the broadcaster's screen. A link will appear — tap it. You will go directly to the viewer page with the code already filled in.
2
Option B — Open the link or type the code
If someone sent you a link, just tap it. Or go to beamo.imazyn.com/viewer.html in any browser and type the 6-character code when asked.
3
The screen appears
Within a few seconds you will see the broadcaster's screen live on your device. Use the buttons at the bottom-right to go fullscreen, mute, or disconnect.
On iPhone: if you see "Tap to unmute", tap anywhere on the screen to enable sound. This is an iPhone security feature.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to things people usually ask.

Why does the "Start Sharing" button not work in Safari?
Apple's Safari browser does not support the screen capture feature needed for broadcasting. Viewers can use Safari, but the person sharing their screen must use Chrome or Edge. Both are free to download.
How many people can watch at the same time?
Up to 50 viewers can watch simultaneously. This covers classrooms, conference rooms, training sessions, and most real-world use cases comfortably.
Does the screen look blurry or laggy?
Quality depends on your internet connection. On a good WiFi or 4G connection, viewers see a sharp, full-resolution stream with less than half a second of delay. On a slow connection, the system automatically adjusts quality to keep things smooth.
Is my screen recorded or stored anywhere?
No. Beamo does not record or store your screen. The video goes directly from your computer to the viewers and is never saved anywhere. The server only handles the connection — not the video itself.
The screen shows "Waiting for broadcaster" — what do I do?
You have joined the room successfully, but the broadcaster has not started sharing yet. Ask them to click "Start Sharing" on their computer. The screen will appear on your device automatically — no need to refresh.
My iPhone says "Tap to unmute" — is something broken?
This is normal on iPhone and iPad. Apple requires a user tap before any video can play with sound. Just tap anywhere on the screen and the audio will turn on immediately.
Do viewers need to be on the same WiFi as the broadcaster?
No. Viewers can be anywhere with an internet connection — same room, different office, or different country. As long as they have the room code or link, they can join.
